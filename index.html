What Is Financial Planning?

Financial planning is planning your money for the future. In financial planning you will organise your money to be in different accounts. They vary based on the person with the most common ones being savings accounts, emergency accounts, investing accounts, salary accounts, retirement accounts and tax saving accounts. These accounts help you stay organised and productive.

People have many steps for financial planning. The most common six are to set up a good and stable finance account and set goals for the future. Individuals then set up insurance, tax returns and more. This allows you to get an idea about your income and your budget.

You will have to have financial goals and categorise them like short, medium and long-term. This helps people work effectively and have productive goals. The most important step is creating a budget. You have to have a budget for investing and for your goals after calculating the cost and debt for long-term and short-term goals.

You also need an emergency fund where you keep a certain amount in options like fixed deposits, high-yield savings accounts, and money market accounts. Investing is key to financial planning, but there are risks and benefits, so safeguarding your investments is the safest option.
